The Journey

Every legend has a beginning. This is the story of the transformation that changed the fitness world forever.

1989

Born in Moscow

Aziz Sergeyevich Shavershian is born on March 24, 1989 in Moscow, Russia. His family would later emigrate to Australia, settling in Sydney.

2004

The WoW Era

A skinny 15-year-old, Aziz spends his days playing World of Warcraft. At just 120 lbs, he's self-conscious about his appearance. His brother Said (Chestbrah) begins training, planting the first seed of change.

2007

The Awakening

Inspired by his brother and the bodybuilding forums (Misc), Aziz picks up his first barbell. The transformation begins. He discovers that the gym is not just about the body — it's about the mind.

2008

The Metamorphosis

Just one year in, the results are staggering. His body begins to take shape. The former WoW addict starts turning heads. He creates the persona "Zyzz" — a character larger than life itself.

2009

The Rise

Zyzz uploads his first YouTube videos. The charisma, the confidence, the physique — everything is magnetic. "U mirin, brah?" becomes the catchphrase of a generation. The Aesthetics Crew is born.

2010

Peak Aesthetics

At 21, Zyzz reaches his peak physique. He becomes the face of aesthetic bodybuilding. The festivals, the hardstyle, the shredded physique under strobe lights — he's living the life he preached. His YouTube videos inspire millions worldwide.

2011

The Legend Solidifies

Zyzz continues to grow his following. He releases training videos, attends events, and becomes an icon. His message is clear: no matter who you are, you can transform yourself. "We're all gonna make it."

Aug 5, 2011

Forever Mirin'

Zyzz passes away in a sauna in Pattaya, Thailand, at just 22 years old. An undiagnosed congenital heart defect claims his life. The fitness world mourns. But legends don't die — they ascend.

2011+

The Eternal Legacy

Zyzz's legacy only grows stronger with time. His videos continue to inspire new generations. "We're all gonna make it" becomes the anthem of self-improvement. The Aesthetics movement lives on, forever.

The Philosophy

Zyzz didn't just change his body — he created an entirely new way of thinking about fitness and self-improvement. While the bodybuilding world was obsessed with mass monsters and powerlifting totals, Zyzz championed something different: aesthetics.

His philosophy was radical in its simplicity. Stop overthinking. Stop caring about what others think. Get in the gym, train hard, eat right, and most importantly — enjoy the process. Life is too short to be a "sad kunt."

"You don't need to have amazing genetics or by by a natural freak of nature. Everyone has the potential to change themselves and live the life they deserve."

He was one of the first fitness influencers before the term even existed. His YouTube videos weren't polished productions — they were raw, authentic, and brimming with genuine enthusiasm. He filmed himself at festivals, in the gym, and just living life. That authenticity is what made him resonate with millions.

The Impact

More than a decade after his passing, Zyzz remains one of the most referenced figures in fitness culture. His transformation photos are shared daily on social media. His quotes have become mantras. His legacy has influenced everything from gym culture to internet memes to music festivals.

Chestbrah (Said Shavershian) continues to carry the torch, keeping his brother's legacy alive through social media and personal appearances. The Zyzz community remains one of the most positive and encouraging corners of the fitness world.

"ZYZZ will never die. He's more than a person — he's an idea, a movement, a reminder that anyone can transform their life if they have the courage to start."
